Chapter 1

Awake from the Slumber

Pre-story

The world had drowned in 2097. Of the 15.6 billion alive before the Great Flood, only 16.3 million of the world's population survived. Humanity had clawed its way back, forging a new life atop the ocean. The crowning achievement of this era was The Hydronova—the largest fully equipped battleship city ever built, a fortress of steel and technology floating on the endless waves. The Hydronova was a marvel of modern engineering—almost as large as New York City and Hundred floors high (400m) and twenty stories under the ocean.

Currently in 2205 - Present!

The four Hydronova's - mega stations ruled the seas. Today, they celebrated the 155th anniversary of the world’s rebirth. Skies buzzed with planes streaking across neon clouds, and streets glimmered with lights and loud fireworks and drone-shows. Almost everyone wore a smile—except for the few old geezers sitting at the top of the observatory station who remembered how bad the catastrophe was back then, in the time of the flood and the horrors it had washed away.

Through the celebrations, with far more light spilling over the ocean than usual, a Drift of Luminox began to emerge beneath Hydronova—massive creatures drawn to the light. we could see a total of 12-14 people starting to surround the Mega station from around.

The top of Hydronova consisted of 180 sectors—the summit of the city, housing all the major technological and medical research labs.

Agent 1: Man, I wish I could take the time off to enjoy the view of the celebrations.

Agent 2: I feel you, but it’s our responsibility to keep the researchers safe from the deep-sea dangers.

Agent 1: I know… still, a little view wouldn’t hurt—never mind.

Suddenly, the feed from Sector 33 flickered, then cut entirely.

Agent 2: Betsy, look at this—the feed just went dark.

Betsy: What! Are you kidding? Those cameras are guarded by a missileproof shield. Even your biggest Luminox would have a hard time breaking it. If someone forced it, we’d see it on the recorded feed. Check it—now!

Agent 2: I was checking that while you were nagging me, but I didn’t see anyone approach the base from below.

Betsy: This sounds like trouble for us, Andre. Sound the alarms and let’s go check it out through our Radeon Glasses.

Betsy and Andre stepped out of the Command Post. They looked down at the exact spot, but there was nothing. Then—a presence.

Explosions erupted across every sector at once, shaking the entire summit of the Hydronova. Alarms screamed through the corridors—so loud they drowned out even the emergency broadcasts. Researchers monitoring the outer-space footage froze mid-communication, panic setting in, before abandoning their posts and rushing down toward the lower decks. Meanwhile, soldiers were already mobilizing, heading for the summit to intercept the invaders.

Rogue began rampaging, tearing through every sector on her path to Sector 67, leaving only blood rain in her wake.

She reached the destination in 1 minute 30 seconds, covering a distance of 270 kilometers—and leaving 3,897 dead. Civilian workers, guards—none survived her dual gratinum swords, sharp enough to slice through a mini hydrogen bomb-resistant door.

She was greeted with one such door—a fortress of reinforced gratinum alloy. With a single swing, her twin blades sliced it in half.

Inside, after killing everyone, she inserted the Paradox into the floating table, projecting a hologram over a massive ice chamber. The system, designed to preserve the subject in perfect suspension, began to thaw.

Rogue: I’m in. The ice is melting. Where are you, Rody?

Rody: Me and Remy—thirty seconds out.

Rogue: Thanks to the Professor, everything’s going according to plan.

Rody: Xenon said there was a hiccup, but he and the others handled it. They’ll be here in three minutes.

Rogue: Good. We should be gone before the Kaedros even realize we’re here.

Remy: Even if they do, it’ll take them twenty to twenty-five minutes from the outer space orbit.

As the ice melted, a man emerged—appearing to be in his thirties. His stance—left leg forward, right bent—frozen mid-swing, clutching a wooden stick.
